Optimising Tourism in Tuscany

The focus of our study is the world-famous region of Central Italy, halfway between Rome and Milan - Tuscany. Home of the beautiful Dome of Florence and the leaning tower of Pisa. With a population of roughly 3.5 million, Tuscany is one of the most popular tourist destinations on earth. It is estimated that around 30 million tourists visited Tuscany in 2017. With this popularity, it is no surprise that tourism accounts for 7% of the region’s GDP, contributing greatly to its economic prosperity and job creation.

This record does not come without challenges though. Severe overcrowding leads to resource depletion, over-usage of infrastructure, and loss of cultural authenticity. Moreover for the locals overcrowding can also have adverse effects, such as increased cost of living and decreased quality of life.

Against this backdrop, Tuscany is seeking a better tourism strategy that balances economic prosperity with social and environmental sustainability for its territories and its citizens. New methods combining data mining, machine learning, and data science are beginning to be used to understand the impact of mass tourism on cities and to find solutions to overtourism.

Data Science for Social Good Europe is a yearly summer initiative bringing together data scientists from all over the world to use such techniques to tackle challenges with a social impact. This past summer 2018, a team of data science fellows conducted research and provided data-driven insights on tourist behaviours and mobility patterns to inform actionable policy aimed at optimal spatial-temporal spread of tourists.

This website aims to provide a tool that can improve the management of crowds and the quality experience for tourists and residents alike.
